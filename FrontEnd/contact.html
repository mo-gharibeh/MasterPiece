Motorcycle maintenance frequency depends on the type of bike, usage, and manufacturer's recommendations, but here are general guidelines:

1. Oil Change: Every 3,000 to 5,000 miles or every 6 months, whichever comes first. Some synthetic oils may last longer.

2. Chain Lubrication and Adjustment: Every 500 miles or after riding in wet or dusty conditions.

3. Tire Pressure and Condition: Check before every ride. Inspect tread wear and look for punctures regularly.

4. Brake Pads: Check every 1,500 to 2,000 miles for wear. Replace if they're thinner than 1/8 inch.
For safe riding, always wear a DOT-approved helmet to protect your head. A motorcycle jacket with armor provides

protection for your torso, while gloves protect your hands and improve grip. Riding pants with padding and motorcycle

boots that cover your ankles help prevent injuries to your legs and feet. Additional gear like eye protection and reflective

clothing also improve safety, especially in low-visibility conditions.
